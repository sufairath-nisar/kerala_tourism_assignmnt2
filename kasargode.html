All About Kasargode

Kasargode

Kanwatheertha Beach

A large formation like a swimming pool in the middle of the sea is the striking feature of Kanwatheertha beach at Manjewaram in Kasaragod district.

Kasargode

Kasaragod, the northernmost state of Kerala, is replete with hills, forts and backwaters. Often described as the land of forts and gods, the place is noted for its rituals.

Malik Dinar Mosque

Believed to have been founded by Persian scholar and traveller Malik Ibin Deenar, this historic mosque itself is a true wonder.

Edneer Mutt

Edneer mutt is one of the most revered places in Kasaragod which is visited by a good number of devotees from Kerala and neighbouring states.

Ranipuram Hills

Switch on the adventurous spirit and head to Ranipuram. The tourist destination in Kasaragod known for its spellbinding beauty doubles up as a picnic spot

Nileswaram - Once the culture centre of Kasaragod

Nileswar, the cultural centre of Kasaragod district, Kerala was the seat of the Nileswar Rajas.

Kundamkuzhy Fort - A Walk to Remember

Located in the Bandadka village in Kasaragod, the Kundamkuzhy Fort is a land fort that dates back to the time of the Bednur Nayaks.

Madiyankulam Durga Temple - famous for Bhutha dance, Hosdurg, Kasaragod

Madiyankulam Durga Temple at Kanhangad, Kasargod district of Kerala is famous for its Bhutha Dance (Pootham).

Madhur Temple

Located eight km northeast of Kasaragod, on the banks of the Madhuvahini River is the Madhur Temple dedicated to Lord Ganapathy.

Rafting on Tejaswini River, Kannur, Kerala, India

Welcome to Cherupuzha in Kannur district in Kerala from where starts the exciting rafting on the River Tejaswini. The river, which is also known by the name Kariamkode

Kappil Beach in Kasaragod

Kappil Beach is situated near the Bekal Fort in Kasaragod district.

Kanwatheertha Beach near Manjeswaram in Kasaragod

Kanwatheertha Beach is ideal for swimming and is situated near Manjeswaram in Kasaragod district.

Chandragiri Fort and River near Bekal in Kasaragod

Chandragiri Fort, situated on the banks of Payaswini River in Kasaragod district, is a 17th century citadel. Chandragiri Boat Club offers speed boat rides, houseboat.

Chithari - a small tropical island and backwaters in Kanhangad, Kasaragod

Chithari is a small tropical island situated 5 km from Kanhangad in Kasaragod district, Kerala. It is covered with lagoons and a sandy beach.

Ranipuram hills in Kasaragod

Ranipuram is located around 85 km from Kasaragod district in Kerala. It is famous for its trekking trails and verdant vegetation.

Bekal Beach in Kasaragod

Bekal is an ideal location in Kasaragod district, Kerala. Situated at about 16 km South of Kasaragod, the imposing fort at Bekal is one of the largest and best preserved.

A cruise through the backwaters of Kottappuram

The Valiyaparamba backwaters, a large body of scenic backwaters is fed by four rivers. The River Tejaswini is one among them and a houseboat cruise through its waters.

The Central Plantation Crops Research Institute, CPCRI, at Kudlu, Kasaragod

The Central Plantation Crops Research Institute (CPCRI) established in 1970 by the Indian Council of Agricultural Research is located at Kudlu in Kasaragod.
